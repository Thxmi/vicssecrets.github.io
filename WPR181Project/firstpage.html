Overview of the World Wide Web

The WWW or World Wide Web is a collection of websites, which are comprised of web pages, which are made up of different types of data. The websites are interconnected through hyperlinks which are used as a source of navigation by the user. It was created by Timothy Berner Lee in 1989 as a means of efficiency through communicating and sharing information.

The WWW consists of multiple components which enable users to view resources or data. The contents or pages of the WWW are stored in different servers across the globe. It is these pages that make up the WWW. As a method of viewing one website or page to the other hyperlinks were created. Hyperlinks are what links the different websites by giving access to one spot through hyperlinks or media, to another website or another page within the website. Hyperlinks define the WWW as an interconnected medium of documents and is a fundamental concept of the W3.

A user should use their client machine to supply a universal identifier to the web server through their browser in order to access the required page. The identifier could either be a URL, universal resource locator, or URI, uniform resource identifier. The website receives the identifier and retrieves the page or media associated with the identifier and presents it to the client machine, the browser.
